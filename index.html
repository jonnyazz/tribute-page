Marilyn Monroe

One of the most popular sex symbol of the 1950s and early 1960s

Here's a timeline of Marilyn Monroe's life

  • 1926 Early life - Born Norma jeane Mortenson at Los Angeles General Hospital.
  • 1930 - Monroe's father's identity is unconfirmed; her mother is confined to asylums. She will spend her childhood in a series of orphanagees and foster homes.
  • 1937 - Moves in with a family friend Grace McKee Goddard.
  • 1942 First marriage - The Goddard family relocates and cannot take Norma Jeane with them. At age 16 Monroe marries 21-year old neighbour Jimmy Dougherty. They divorced soon after World War ll
  • 1945 Modeling career - Discovered by U.S.Army photographer David Conover. First shoot with Andre de Dienes.
  • 1946 - First national magazine cover, FAMILY CIRCLE by Andre de Dienes. Signs first contract with 20th Century Fox as Marilyn Monroe.
  • 1949 - Poses for nude calender shots taken by Tom Kelley. Tobey Beach,Long Island shoot with Andre de Dienes.
  • 1950 Big Break - ASPHALT JUNGLE released.
  • 1952 - First Life magazine cover, shot by Philippe Halsman.
  • 1953 Iconic films - Handprints on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. GENTLEMEN PREFER BLONDES and HOW TO MARRY A MILLIONAIRE released. Becomes the first PLAYBOY centerfold.
  • 1954 Second marriage - Marries Joe DiMaggio. Performs for troops in Korea. Ballerina sitting with Milton Greene. Shoots iconic skirt blowing scene for THE SEVEN YEAR ITCH.
  • 1955 - Studies at the Actors Studio. Rides the pink elephant in the pink elephant in the circus at Madison Square Garden.Interviewed by Edward R.Morrow. THE SEVEN YEAR ITCH released.
  • 1956 Third marriage - Marries Arthur Miller and is photographed with him by Sam Shaw. BUS STOP released.
  • 1957 - THE PRINCE AND THE SHOWGIRL released.
  • 1958 - Phil Stern photographs a pregnent Marilyn in the MGM lot.
  • 1959 - SOME LIKE IT HOT released.
  • 1960 - Films THE MISFITS on locations in Reno, Nevada. Magnum photograhers exclusively shoot the cast.
  • 1961 - THE MISFITS released. Douglas Kirkland's bed sitting with Marilyn.
  • 1962 - Photographed with Carl Sandberg. Sings Happy Birthday Mr. President to JFK. The "Last Sitting" with Bert Stern for Vogue magazine. Last Interview, for LIFE magazine. Dies at 36.
